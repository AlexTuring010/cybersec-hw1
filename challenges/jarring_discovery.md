The plan was simple: drop in, grab the treasure of Jar Jar Binks, and get out. But fate, as always, had other plans. The landing? More like a crash. The jungle? Dense, foggy, and full of secrets.

Somehow, among the wreckage, one thing survived—`JarringDiscovery.jar`.

I’d seen files like this before—JARs packed with Java bytecode. Dangerous in the wrong hands, or in the right jungle. I cracked it open with:

```bash
jar xf JarringDiscovery.jar
```

And inside? A map. Not a literal one, but something tucked away in the `resources`—a path. A series of choices, hidden in plain sight. The jungle wanted me to play its game.

I ran the file:

```bash
java -jar JarringDiscovery.jar
```

And I was dropped into the story.

A river. A split path. The fog thick as soup.

I chose to **walk downstream (3)**. The jungle grew darker.

Next, I searched for **higher ground (4)**, but it was all flat. That’s when I met the snake. Big. Black. Hissing.

I did the only thing that made sense: **charged it (1)**.

I dodged its strike and ran until the trees opened up. The ground grew rocky—promising. I **brushed aside the undergrowth (2)** and got ambushed by spiders. But behind them, I found the cave.

Inside, four treasures waited. But I knew better. The map had whispered to me. I **took the fourth treasure (4)**.

The cave shook. Collapse. No way out—unless I gave something up. I **placed my bag on the pedestal (3)**. A door opened.

Back outside, lost again in the haze. I **headed back the way I came (2)**, trusting my memory of the stream.

That’s when I saw it.

Etched into the treasure:

```
flag{fri3ndsh1p_1s_th3_tru3_tre4sur3_dc69360c5a92cc948bd096cf48956c33}
```

Could it be a trick? Maybe. But the path was right. The choices, true. I escaped with my life—and the flag.
